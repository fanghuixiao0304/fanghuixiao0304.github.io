Fanghui Xiao
I'm a Ph.D. candidate in the School of Computing and Information (SCI) at the University of Pittsburgh. I study under my advisor Prof. Daqing He who leads the iRiS lab. I am part of a team of active researchers who each research diverse areas, such as human-centered design, machine learning, and information access. Prior to entering the Ph.D. program, I earned my master's degree in Library and Information Science at the University of Pittsburgh. Before that, I worked for Baidu, a mega internet technology company, as a project manager and training specialist.
My research is at the intersection of human-computer interaction (HCI), human data Interaction (HII), and data science. My Ph.D. research focuses on exploring user behaviors of interacting with open data and the ever-changing technologies, understanding their experiences and challenges, and ultimately, designing novel technologies that address the challenges. I employ mixed research methods to conduct most of my research studies, such as interviews, content analysis, surveys, and a large-scale data analysis.
I am passionate about working on research that aim to understand user insights and behaviors, to improve users' experience in diverse domains by means of novel designs, and finally, to cogently support the corresponding user and research communities.
